History of Video Games on Linux

The Origins (1950-1970)

Video games were born as scientific experiments in universities and laboratories. One of the earliest examples was the Cathode Ray Tube Amusement Device (1947), a missile simulation game. In the 1950s, scientists like William Higinbotham created games such as Tennis for Two (1958), a tennis simulator on an oscilloscope.

In 1962, Spacewar! was developed at MIT by Steve Russell and is considered one of the first digital video games. This space combat game inspired future innovations in the industry.

The First Generation of Consoles and Arcades (1970-1980)

The 1970s saw the arrival of the first home consoles and arcade machines.

  • In 1972, the Magnavox Odyssey became the first home console, designed by Ralph Baer.
  • That same year, Atari released Pong, created by Nolan Bushnell and Al Alcorn, the first commercial video game success in arcades.
  • By the late 70s, classics like Space Invaders (1978) and Asteroids (1979) emerged, solidifying the video game industry.

The Golden Age of Video Games (1980-1990)

This decade saw the massive expansion of video games with iconic franchises:

  • Pac-Man (1980) by Namco, one of the most influential games of all time.
  • Donkey Kong (1981) by Nintendo, where Mario made his debut.
  • The 1983 video game crash occurred due to market saturation and low-quality games.
  • In 1985, Nintendo released the Nintendo Entertainment System (NES), with titles like Super Mario Bros., reviving the industry and marking the beginning of modern franchises.

The 16-Bit Revolution and 3D (1990-2000)

The 90s brought major technological advancements:

  • The Super Nintendo (SNES) and Sega Genesis (Mega Drive) improved game quality.
  • Street Fighter II (1991) and Mortal Kombat popularized fighting games.
  • Sony entered the market with the PlayStation (1994), introducing the CD-ROM as a standard.
  • In 1996, Nintendo launched the Nintendo 64 with Super Mario 64, revolutionizing 3D gaming.

The Modern Era and Online Gaming (2000-Present)

With the sixth generation of consoles, the industry focused on advanced graphics and online gaming:

  • PlayStation 2 (2000), the best-selling console of all time.
  • Xbox (2001) introduced Xbox Live, boosting online multiplayer.
  • World of Warcraft (2004) popularized MMORPGs.
  • Nintendo Wii (2006) introduced motion controls.
  • Today, video games have evolved with virtual reality (VR), streaming, and the rise of eSports.
